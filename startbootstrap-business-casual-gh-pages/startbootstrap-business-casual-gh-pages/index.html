COIR FIBER Uses/Applications

COIR is a versatile natural fiber extracted from the mesocarp tissue, or husk of the coconut fruit, and the fiber is golden in color when cleaned after removal from the coconut shell; Hence the name "The Golden Fiber".

Fibrous husks are soaked in slow moving water in pits or nets to swell and soften the fibers. The long bristle fibers are separated from the short mat fibers beneath the nut's skin in a process known as wet milling. Some mattress fiber is allowed to retain more moisture so that it retains its elasticity for 'twisted' fiber production. Coir is elastic enough to twist without breaking and it holds a curl. The long bristle fiber is washed in clean water and then dried before being tied into bundles or hunks. It can be cleaned and 'hacked' with steel combs to straighten the fibers and remove small fiber fragments. Yarn bristle fiber can be bleached and dyed to obtain different colored hanks.

Coco coir is great for hydroponic systems as well!

Coconut coir is a natural, organic material formed of coco fiber that can be used as a potting medium in your garden. For example, you can use pure coconut husk to grow orchids, as part of the soil mixture, and as mulch to protect plants or just make them seem nicer.

Best for all plants the combination of balanced pH, low natural salt and minimal odor makes these coconut husk chips ideal for growing all plants. It mixes well with soil and is ideal for home garden and commercial growing.
