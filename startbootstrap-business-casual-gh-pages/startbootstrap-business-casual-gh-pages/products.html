COIR FIBER

A natural fiber extracted from coconut husks and used in products such as carpets, door mats, brushes and cushions. Coconut husk is the hard, fibrous material found between the inner shell and the outer coat of the coconut. Other uses of brown coir (made from ripe coconuts) are in upholstery padding, redundancy and gardening.

White coir extracted from unripe coconuts is used to make fine brushes, string, rope and fishing nets. It has the advantage of not sinking, so it can be used for long stretches in deep water without dragging heavy boats and floats. COIR HUSK

Coco chips are also used as a mulching agent as well as a soil conditioner. We are the Coconut Husked Chips Manufacturer and Expoters in india. Our coir chips are used as mulching material in gardens. It is a natural and renewable substitute for pine bark.

A garden where coir is applied does not only look beautiful but on top of that is better for plants and trees because of its property to retain high amounts of water and to release it there where necessary.

COIR PITH

Coco-peat blocks are considered an ideal growing medium. The cocopeat powder thus obtained is sieved, washed, dried and finally compressed into blocks.

The blocks can be used for a wide range of planting requirements. Coir is known for its natural rooting hormones and anti-fungal properties.

We also ensure secure packaging of the coco-peat blocks. The blocks are stretch wrapped and strapped. We also customise the packing on client request.
